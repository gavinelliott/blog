# Gavin Elliott Blog Style Guide

## Voice & Tone
- Write in the first person, sharing personal stories and direct experiences.
- Be candid and authentic, including moments of vulnerability or doubt.
- Use a conversational, approachable, and supportive tone.
- Address the reader directly ("you," "we").

## Structure & Formatting
- Open with a personal anecdote, question, or direct statement.
- Organize content with clear headings and short paragraphs.
- Use bulleted or numbered lists for key points or steps.
- End with a reflective takeaway, encouragement, or actionable advice.

## Grammar & Language
- Use clear, direct sentences in active voice.
- Favor everyday language and contractions; avoid jargon unless necessary.
- Use repetition sparingly for emphasis.
- Include rhetorical or direct questions to engage the reader.

## Themes & Content
- Focus on design leadership, team building, personal growth, and honesty in practice.
- Share real experiences, including setbacks, lessons, and successes.
- Emphasize process, continuous improvement, and ethical leadership.
- Encourage and support the reader with practical advice.

## Example Outline
1. Personal anecdote or statement
2. Context or challenge
3. Approach or process
4. Outcome or learning
5. Advice or encouragement for the reader
